4 AND 5 TOES

Prairie Dog

Cynomys spp.
To 16 in. (40 cm)
Stocky, yellow-brown rodent
has a white- or black-tipped
tail. Hind tracks are about 1.5
in. (4 cm) long with 5 toes.
Front tracks show 4 toes.

Woodchuck

Marmota monax
To 32 in. (80 cm)
Large, stocky rodent has
brown fur grizzled with white.
Hind tracks are about 2.5 in.
(6 cm) long and have 5 toes.
Front tracks show 4 toes.

Yellow-bellied
Marmot

Marmota flaviventris
To 28 in. (70 cm)
Large, brownish rodent
has a yellowish belly
and a long, bushy tail.
Hind tracks are about
2.5 in. (6 cm) long and
have 5 toes. Front
tracks show 4 toes.

Pocket Gopher

Thomomys spp.
To 11 in. (28 cm)
Chunky, burrowing rodent
has small eyes and crescent-
shaped claws. Tracks are not
usually evident. Best signs are
earth mounds featuring an
earth plug in a depression
marking the entrance.

Eastern Mole

Scalopus aquaticus
To 9 in. (23 cm)
Stout, brownish mammal
has large front feet
with out-turned claws.
Best signs are raised
ridges of earth leading to
lumpy dirt mounds that
lack an entrance.

Common Porcupine

Erethizon dorsatum
To 3 ft. (90 cm)
Chunky mammal is told by
its hunched back and long
coat of barbed quills. Hind
tracks are 3-4 in. (8-10 cm)
long. Note straight toes.

Nine-banded Armadillo

Dasypus novemcinctus
To 32 in. (80 cm)
Distinguished by its coat of
bony plates. Distinctive
tracks have 5 toes on
the hind foot and 4
on the front foot
and are 1-2 in.
(3-5 cm) long.
