4 TOES

Eastern Cottontail
Sylvilagus floridanus
To 18 in. (45 cm)
Gray-brown bunny has a
reddish nape and a white tail.
Hind tracks are 3-4 in.
(8-10 cm) long. Front track is
smaller and rounder.

Snowshoe Hare
Lepus americanus
To 20 in. (50 cm)
Coat is gray-brown in summer,
white in winter. Hind tracks are
5-6 in. (13-15 cm) long and wider
than those of the cottontail.

Jackrabbit
Lepus spp.
To 25 in. (63 cm)
Large gray or tan rabbit has
large, black-tipped ears and a
black-streaked tail. Hind prints
are 2-3 in. (5-8 cm) long. Track
sets may be over 12 ft. (3.6 m)
apart when running.

Bobcat
Lynx rufus
To 4 ft. (1.2 m)
Large cat has spotted red-brown
coat and a short tail that is
black-striped on top. Rounded
tracks are about 2 in. (5 cm)
across and lack claw marks.

Lynx
Lynx lynx
To 42 in. (1.06 m)
Key field marks are tufted ears
and black-tipped tail. Rounded
tracks are 3-4 in. (8-10 cm)
across and lack claw marks.
Fore print is slightly larger.

Mountain Lion
Puma concolor
To 9 ft. (2.7 m)
Large tan cat has a whitish
belly and a long, black-
tipped tail. Rounded tracks
are 3-4 in. (8-10 cm)
across and lack claws. Fore
print is slightly larger.

Red Fox
Vulpes vulpes
To 40 in. (1 m)
Distinguished by its rusty coat
and white-tipped tail. Tracks
are about 2 in. (5 cm) long
and the claws are visible. Heel
pad has shape of an inverted V.
Feet are hairy and tracks may
be blurred in snow or mud.

Gray Fox
Urocyon cinereoargenteus
To 3.5 ft. (1.1 m)
Coat is black-gray above and
whitish below. Tail has black tip.
Tracks are 1-1.5 in. (3-4 cm)
long and the claws are visible.
Feet are not hairy and toe pads
leave distinct impressions.
