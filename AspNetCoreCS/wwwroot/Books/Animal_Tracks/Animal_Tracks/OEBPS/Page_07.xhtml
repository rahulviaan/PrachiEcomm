4 AND 5 TOES

  Chipmunk

Tamias spp.
To 12 in. (30 cm)
Distinguished by the dark
and light stripes on its
face and back. Hind tracks
are about 1.5 in. (4 cm) long
and have 5 toes. Front tracks
are smaller and show 4 toes.

Gray Squirrel

Sciurus spp.
To 20 in. (50 cm)
Grayish squirrel has a
bushy tail. Black variants
also exist. Hind tracks are
about 2.5 in. (6 cm) long
and have 5 toes.

Fox Squirrel

Sciurus niger
To 28 in. (70 cm)
Large squirrel is gray-black
above and light or yelloworange
below. Hind tracks
are 2-3 in. (5-8 cm) long
and have 5 toes.

Thirteen-lined
Ground Squirrel

Spermophilus tridecemlineatus
To 12 in. (30 cm)
Slender, burrowing squirrel has
dark brown stripes on its back
interspersed with light spots.
Hind tracks are 1-2 in. (3-5 cm)
in length with 5 long toes.

Red Squirrel

Tamiasciurus hudsonicus
To 14 in. (35 cm)
Squirrel is rusty red above
and whitish below. Hind
tracks are 1-2 in. (3-5 cm)
long with 5 toes.

Golden-mantled
Ground Squirrel

Callospermophilus lateralis
To 12 in. (30 cm)
Chipmunk-like, ground-
dwelling squirrel has light
side stripe and lacks facial
stripes. Hind tracks are
about 1 in. (3 cm) long
and have 5 toes.

Northern
Flying Squirrel

Glaucomys sabrinus
To 14 in. (35 cm)
Gray-brown squirrel has a
loose fold of skin between
its front and back legs. By
stretching its flight skin
taut, it can glide between
trees. Hind tracks are
about 1.5 in. (4 cm) long
and have 5 toes.
