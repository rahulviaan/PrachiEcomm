4 TOES

Coyote

Canis latrans
To 52 in. (1.3 m)
Yellow-gray dog has a
bushy, black-tipped tail.
Tracks are about 2 in.
(5 cm) long and claw
marks are visible. Fore
print is slightly larger
than the hind print.
Toe pads are wider than
those of the fox.

Gray Wolf

Canis lupus
To 6.5 ft. (2 m)
Large dog usually has a
gray to black coat and a
black-tipped tail. Robust
tracks are 5-6 in. (13-15
cm) long and up to 5 in.
(13 cm) wide. Toe pads
are large.

5 TOES

Long-tailed Weasel

Mustela frenata
To 21 in. (53 cm)
Slender mammal has short
legs and a black-tipped tail.
Coat may turn white in
winter. Rounded tracks
are about 1 in. (3 cm)
across and often only
show 4 of the 5 toes.

Mink

Neovison vison
To 28 in. (70 cm)
Told by its sleek brown
coat and white chin patch.
Tracks are about 1.5 in.
(4 cm) across and claws are
visible. In snow, minks and
weasels leave distinctive
sets of tracks consisting of
paired depressions.

Striped Skunk

Mephitis mephitis
To 32 in. (80 cm)
Coat is black with
2 wide white stripes
down the back. Hind
print is 2-3 in. (5-8 cm) long;
fore print is 1-2 in. (3-5 cm)
long. Claws are often only
evident on fore prints.

American Badger

Taxidea taxus
To 35 in. (88 cm)
Stocky, burrowing animal
has black-and-white stripes
on its head and back. Tracks
are 2-3 in. (5-8 cm) long.
Claw marks are longer on
the fore print.
